Beta Open
// Official Rules · Version 1.0
PolitiPlay
Read them once. You'll know the game.
I
The Sweep
Every week a Sweep opens. It closes Sunday at midnight. Your squad scores points. The highest score at your table wins.
A new draft opens while the current Sweep is still scoring. Build next week's squad any time before Friday midnight — or just watch this week's board move. Wednesday through Sunday is scoring time. Wednesday through Friday is draft and swap time.
II
The Squad
Draft ten real political figures. Two from each position — HoS, LEG, OPP, WBR, WLD. One figure per slot. No repeats.
HoS = Head of State · LEG = Legislator · OPP = Opposition · WBR = World Body · WLD = Kibitzers & Criers. See How To Play for the full position guide.
III
Scoring
When your figures drive headlines, you score. Bigger headlines, more points. Every score is sourced, verified, and linked. Click any event to see the evidence.
Points are awarded based on news cycle impact — volume, velocity, and reach. A figure scores on every event that touches them during the Sweep, regardless of which role they acted in. A head of state who also moves markets scores twice.
IV
Kibitzers & Criers
Your two Wildcard slots belong to the Kibitzers and Criers. Both earn 1.5× on every point scored. The news decides which one matters this week.
Kibitzers are the formerly powerful whose opinions still carry weight — the exiled, the sidelined, the influential who move rooms from outside them. Criers carry less institutional weight but unpredictable consequence — they can go from irrelevant to unavoidable overnight. Both are volatile. Both are dangerous.
V
The Ownership Rule
The more players at your table who draft the same figure, the less that figure scores for all of them. The game is won in the picks others missed.
Ownership dilution applies per table, not globally. A figure owned by 80% of your table scores at 68% of base value for those players. A figure only you drafted scores at full value. Consensus is taxed. Contrarian insight is rewarded.
VI
Your Table
You compete at a table of ten. Not against the world — against nine people who think they read the room just as well as you do.
Your squad is private until the Sweep opens. Once scoring begins, all ten squads at your table are fully visible to everyone. Watch what your opponents drafted. Watch who's scoring. The table chat is open. That's the game.
VII
The Swap
You get one swap per Sweep. The swap window closes Friday at midnight — 48 hours before the Sweep ends. After that your roster is locked until Sunday.
The biggest news usually breaks when you can no longer do anything about it. That's the game. The swap is your one correction — use it on knowledge you had but didn't fully act on at draft time, not as a reaction to events already in motion.
VIII
Multi-Register Scoring
A figure scores on every event that touches them during the Sweep. Draft the figures who have more than one way to make news.
A head of state who also controls a sovereign wealth fund scores on both governmental actions and financial moves. A Kibitzer who appears in a document leak and gives a viral interview in the same week scores on both. Power operates in multiple registers simultaneously.
IX
The Court of Public Opinion
After each Sweep, the most consequential events go to the Court of Public Opinion. Your table votes on their true weight — positive or negative. The Court runs a separate ledger.
The Court measures something the score alone cannot: whether you understand not just what happened, but what it meant. Your Court record accumulates across the season and is part of your permanent player profile. Season 2.
X
The Truth
The score is the truth. Your ranking is honest. The news decides — not your opinion of the news, not your politics, not your preferences.
You don't argue with the score. You don't argue with the news. You learn to read the room better than the nine people sitting at your table. That's the only game being played here.
