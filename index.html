// The game

How it works

1
Take your seat
You're assigned to a table of ten. These are your opponents — nine people who think they read the political room just as well as you do. Your seat is yours for the season.
2
Draft your squad
Pick ten real figures into five positions — HoS, LEG, OPP, WBR, WLD — two per slot. From a catalog of 750+ world leaders, legislators, and Kibitzers & Criers.
3
Score the cycle
When your figures drive headlines, you score. Every point is editorially reviewed, sourced, and linked. One swap before Friday midnight. Sweep closes Sunday. Best read wins.
// Why it matters

This isn't just a game.
It's a judgment arena.

Everyone has opinions about politics. Most people never have to back them up with anything. Politiplay changes that. Every draft pick is a public statement of belief — who's going to move, who's going to matter, who's about to blow up the cycle.

"Your political instincts, tested weekly against the room and against reality."

The news doesn't lie. When Trump signs an executive order, it scores. When Starmer files a no-confidence motion, it scores. When a Kibitzer surfaces in a document leak and scores 22 points nobody saw coming — that's the player who was paying attention. The score reflects the news cycle — adjudicated against Politiplay's published rules, sourced, and linked.
